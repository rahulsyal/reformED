It's time to change Newark's School System

In 2012, NJ Public Schools Superintendent Cami Anderson announced the "Renew Schools" program for 8 elementary schools. It was a failure.

What's the Issue?

In two years, the "Renew Schools" program was supposed to bring profiency rates on the NJ ASK to 50%.

"Renew schools have a target of 50 percent proficiency rate within two years (emphasis added), 75 percent in four years, an increase from the current average of 20 percent." - Superintendent Anderson, 2012

Superintendent Anderon proposed the following as part of the Renew Schools initiative:

  • Lengthening the school day by one hour
  • Appointing administrative positions directly from the Renew Schools Board
  • Having principals be responsible for hiring their respective teaching staff
  • Requiring students to wear school uniforms

Let's breakdown the four major changes.


Superintendent Anderson pushed these major changes through the school system without consulting Newark's residents or Board of Education. This created a division between the children/residents of Newark and the Renew Schools Board. Now's the time for transparency!

So, what is the general consensus on the implemented changes? Click on the panels on the right to see!

Lengthening the school day by an hour

A longer school day does not automatically result in a more productive day. Teaching methods and modes of delivery stayed the same. Even with the extra hour, students and teachers agreed that the same amount of content was introduced - it was just stretched out.

Appointing school administrators directly through the Renew Schools Board

Bypassing the teachers, principals, and Board of Education led to a disconnect between the said entities and the school administrators. As the administrators had to follow Renew Schools' agenda, a conflict of interest was introduced.

Having principals solely responsible for hiring their teaching staff

Without incorporating the input of other teachers, there was a lack of unity amongst the teaching staff. Without the teachers acting as a unit, significant disconnects in the curriculum arose between different classes and material.

Requiring students to wear school uniforms

School uniforms have their advantages and disadvantages. Advantages include keeping students focused on their work instead of clothes as well as reducing peer pressure and bullying. Disadvantages include restricting students' creativity and promoting conformity over individuality.

Analysis of Reports on the Renew Schools Program

Not one of the eight Renew Schools met the target of 50 percent proficiency rate within two years on either the Langauge Arts or Math portions of the NJ ASK.

Profiency rates DECREASED during the duration of the program. The new program had a NEGATIVE impact on students' performance on the NJ ASK.
